What are Seismic Waves?

An earthquake is the perceptible shaking of the surface of the ground, caused by the sudden breaking and movement of Earth's rocky outermost crust creating seismic waves. Earthquakes causes tremendous human life loss as a result of collapsing building and structures.

Seisometers, accelerometers or hydrophones (inside water bodies) are used to measure the intensity of Earthquake.Seismology is the scientific study of earthquakes and the propagation of elastic waves through the Earth or through other planet-like bodies.
